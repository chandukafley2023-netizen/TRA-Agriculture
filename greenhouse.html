The North Wing Greenhouses

Welcome to the core of our agricultural technology program. Our twin greenhouses are climate-controlled, soil-free environments designed to maximize yield, minimize water usage, and serve as a living laboratory for TRA students.

Our Hydroponic Systems

🌊

Deep Water Culture (DWC)

In our DWC beds, plant roots are suspended directly in highly oxygenated, nutrient-rich water. This system is incredibly fast-growing and water-efficient, making it perfect for our leafy greens.

Sensor Monitored
🚰

Nutrient Film Technique (NFT)

NFT uses shallow PVC channels to flow a very thin "film" of water constantly past the plant roots. This ensures the plants get exactly the nutrients they need while exposing the upper roots to abundant oxygen.

Automated Flow
🍅

Dutch Bucket System

For large, vining plants like tomatoes and cucumbers, we use heavy-duty buckets filled with a lightweight medium (like clay pebbles). A drip emitter feeds each plant individually.

Drip Irrigation

Butterhead Lettuce

Our staple crop. Grown in the DWC beds, this lettuce goes from seedling to full harvest in just under 35 days. It thrives in our targeted 22°C water temperature.

🌿

Genovese Basil

Housed in our NFT channels, our basil provides a highly aromatic yield. Hydroponic basil grows exceptionally fast because it never has to fight soil resistance.

Alpine Strawberries

An experimental crop for the club this semester! We are carefully tweaking the Potassium and Phosphorus (EC) levels in the reservoir to encourage sweet, continuous fruiting.
